Scarcity of quality

Home All posts Thoughts Papers

Lately I concentrated my efforts on optimizing or descovering new mental problem solving tools.

Curently I am focusing on philosophy(mainly descriptive), logic(the main tool) and mathematics(a more pragmatic branch of logic that helps me in everyday life).

I feel that the sharper my tools will be, the more I could spot logical fallacies or inconsistencies, which in turn will make me lose appreciation in everyday events. If I spot multiple inconsistencies in a movie’s script, although it was highly entertaining, the movie is bad.

Suspension of disbelief is a great tool, but if a movies logic is inconsistent even in the movie’s internal universe, its a bad movie. This will make me appreciate more a quality movie, but those are rare and far between.

Maybe math will quench my thirst for logical consistency. Maybe art will can be the only substitute for mundane entertainment. Maybe.
