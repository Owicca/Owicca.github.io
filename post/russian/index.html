General Grammar:

  • End of word consonants are pronounced like their voiceless counterparts:
    • б п
    • в ф
    • г к
    • д т
    • ж ш
    • з с
  • Consonants are voiceless when followed by other voiceless consonants and the other way around
  • Plurals are built with infinitive + ‘и’/‘ь’
  • ‘что’ is used for alive objects, and ‘кто’ for inanimate objects.
  • inflection means the endings of the words change according with the grammar of the sentence
  • Gender:
    • masculine: ending in a consonant or й, ёнок, онок, тель
    • feminine: ending in a, я or ия, ость
    • neuter: ending in o, e, ие or ия
    • other:
      • ending in ь can be either masculine or feminine
      • gender of animate nouns is defined by the natural sex of a person or animal:
        • мальчик is always masculine
        • девочка is always feminine
  • in ий and ый the й is inaudible
  • я before the stress sounds like е
  • all imperative forms like здравствуйте drop the те when you’re speaking to someone you call ты

Stress:

  • if an word contains an ё the stress always falls on ё
  • if the infinitive of a verb is stressed on any syllable except the last one, the stress is fixed
  • if the infinitive is stressed on the last syllable, all the other forms will be stressed on the last syllable OR all the other forms will be stressed on the stem

Noun/Adjective Cases:

  • Nominative
  • Accusative:
    • usage:
      • after verbs,where you would use ‘him’ not ‘he’
      • in time expressions of duration and frequency:
        • подаждите минуту: wait a minute
        • я здесь уже неделю: i have been here for a week
        • раз в год: once a year
      • with the prepositions через(through) and за(for)
      • to express motion to somewhere with в and на
    • nouns ending in а change it to у and nouns ending in я change it to ю
    • masculine animate nouns:
      • ending in a consonant,add а
      • ending in a soft like ь or й, replace with я
    • posesive forms: m|f|n —:|—:|–: мой|моя|моё твой[tvai]|твоя[tvaia]|твоё его[ievo]|| её|| его[ievo]|| наш|наша|наше ваш|ваша|ваше их||
    • pronoun forms: меня, тебя, его[ievo], её, его[ievo], нас, вас, них
  • Genitive
  • Dative
  • Instrumental
  • Prepositional:
    • after prepositions в, на, о and при, most masculine nouns add е, and most feminine nouns change last а, й or я to е
    • if the noun ends in ь, if it is masculine it changes to е, if it is feminine it changes to и
    • pronoun forms:
      я мне
      ты тебе
      он нём
      она нёи
      оно нём
      мы нас
      вы вас
      они них
    • indeclinable nouns:
      • words ending in и, у or ю are neuter
      • female names ending with a consonant(or anything other than а, я or ь)(?!?!??!)
      • foreign words ending in о are neuter
    • for nouns that end in я or е and the letter precedding them is и, the ending is a second и
    • those few nouns that, in nominative end in ий, become ии
    • some masculine nouns change ending to у(stressed) when preceeded by в or на
    • this case does not mean anything, its just an grammatical ending
    • prepositions are read as if joined with the following work, so a в before an unvoiced consonant will become itself ф, an unvoiced consonant
    • что is also used as a join clause signifying that Она говорит, что ваня в Сибири
    • чём is the prepositional case for что
