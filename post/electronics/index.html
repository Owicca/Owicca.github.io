foundations:

  • voltage(V or E):
    • cost of energy required to move a unit of positive charge from lower potential point to the higher potential point
    • voltage is what you apply to cause currents to flow
    • [!] a joule(J) of work is done in moving a coloumb(C, unit of electric charge) of charge through a potential difference of 1V
  • current(I):
    • rate of flow of electronic charge past a point
    • measured by ampere/amperes/amp(A)
    • [!] 1A = flow of 1C of charge per second
    • [!] current through a metalic conductor is proportional to the voltage across it
  • V and I rules:
    1. [!] the sum of curents into a point is equal to sum of curents out if it
    2. [!] things hooked in parallel have the same voltage between them(Kirchhoff’s voltage law, KVL)
    3. [!] power:
      • [!] the power(energy per unit time) consumed by a circuit is: P = V * I
      • that is the formula for “average” power, or at an instant in time, because V and I in a circuit can variy periodically
      • P is measured in W(watts)
      • [!] 1W = 1J/s
      • 60W = 120 * I => I = 60/120 => I = 0.5 A
  • rezistance and resistors:
    • resistor:
      • convert V to I
      • I proportional to V
      • R(resistance) is measured in ohms
      • [!] Ohm’s law: R = V / I
      • defects:
        • variations in resistance with temperature, voltage, time and humidity
        • variations in inductance at high frequencies
        • they get hot in power applications
        • noise generation in low-noise amplifiers
    • capacitor: I proportional to the rate of change of V
    • diode: I flows in only one direction
    • thermistor: temperature-dependent resistor
    • photoresistor: light-dependent resistor
    • strain gauges: strain-dependent resistor
    • [!] transistor: the current that can flow between a pair of terminals is determined by the voltage applied to a third terminal
