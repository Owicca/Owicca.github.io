Letter pronunciation:

  • odd letters:
    • g: hei(gutural h)
    • h: ha
    • j: iei
    • q: cu
    • w: uei
  • vowel combinations(diphtongs):
    • ij:
      • normally “ai”
      • inside -lijk: “uh”
    • ou and au: “au”
    • oe: “uu”
    • ui: “au”
    • eu: “eou”
    • eeuw / ieuw: “eeu” / “ieu”
  • consonant combinations:
    • ch: g or “ș”
    • sj: “ș”
    • sch: sg
    • ng: nasal “nnnn”
  • misc:
    • c can be pronounced as either s or k
    • i:
      • short as “e”
      • long as “i”
      • when in words ending with -ig or -is: “ă”
    • q:
      • only used in foreign words followed by u: “kuh”
    • v:
      • prononunced quite close to f
    • w(“uei”):
      • when followed by r it is pronounced v: wraak
    • y(y-grec):
      • as i: xylofoon
      • as j: rayon
    • words never end in double consonants: rennen becomes ik ren
    • words that end in a long vowel, double it: lopen becomes wie loopen
    • words that end in v or z transform it in f and s
    • letters that swap pronunciation at the end of the word:

  • tenses:
    • present tense:
      • verbs followed by jij or je drop the ending -t
      • verb ending for u is always -t
      • verbs with stems that end in -t or -d:
        • -t do not add another t
        • -d add a t or drop the -d when inverted(followed by jij or je)
      • irregular infinitives:
        • some one-syllable verbs have a stem ending in simply -n
      • spelling in verb conjugation

  • modal verbs:
    • can(kunnen), should(zullen), may(mogen), must(moeten), want to(willen), etc
    • modal verbs are conjucated normally and replace the main verb in the phrase, the main verb is the added at the end(as the last word) in infinitive form
    • there are forms where the infinitive main verb is not said: “Wilt u loopt? ja, ik wil (lopen)”

  • phrase types:
    • simple:
      • subject - verb - the rest
      • “Ik heb helenmaal geen kleingeld”
    • inverted:
      • the phrase is a direct question or request
      • verb - subject - the rest
      • “Morgens drink ik koffie”

      • when there is an conjunctive word connecting two phrases: “dat”
      • all the verbs at the end of the sentence
      • the order of the verbs at the end is not important

random grammar:

  • naar, aan, om:

    • naar: towards
    • aan: onto
    • om: on

  • e at the end of an adjective:

    • remove e when there is no noun after the adjective: Dit soep is heet.
    • remove e when the noun is indefinite: Ik lees een dik boek.

  • when to use alleen, maar or pas:

    • alleen:
      • nothing else than
      • only person
    • maar:
      • not more than(in combination with a quantity)
    • pas:
      • not until
      • not long ago

verb conjugation elements:

  • mood:
    • indicative
    • conditional
    • imperative
  • voice:
    • active
    • passive
  • tense:

    • list:

      1. present:
        • usage:
          • an action that happens now
          • a current continous action, an habitual action
          • when talking about a current state
          • conditional ‘if - then’ situation
        • rules:
          • singular
            • first: infinitive stem
            • second: infinitive stem + t
            • third: infinitive stem + t
          • plurar:
            • any person: infinitive stem + en
          • u: infinitive stem
          • second person inversion to Verb-Subject: infinitive stem
        • example:
          • Ik werk moeilijk: I work hard
          • Wil jij met ik spelen?: Do you want to play with me?
      2. present perfect:
        • usage:
          • sets the event in the past, then simple past describes the event
          • can be interchangeably used with simple past
        • rules:
          • hebben/zijn + (ge + past tense conjugation)
          • no verb adds ge if it already starting with a be, er, ge, her, ont, ver
        • examples:
          • Ik heb moeilijk gewerkt: I have been working hard.
      3. past:
        • usage:
          • an event that took place in the past and does not hold any relation to the prezent
          • when we prezent a past action by the verb toen(then)
        • rules:
          • weak verbs:
            • use t when infinitive stem ends in: p, t, k, f, s, ch
            • singular:
              • any person: infinitive stem + t/d + e
            • plural:
              • any person: infinitive stem + t/d + en
          • strong verbs:
            • infinitive stem = internal vowel change
            • singular:
              • any person: infinitive stem
            • plural:
              • any person: infinitive stem + en
          • irregular: rote memorisation
        • example:
          • De Industriele Revolutie begon in Engeland.: The Industrial Revolution started in England.
          • Ik heb gemaakt.: I made.
      4. past perfect:
        • usage:
          • zullen:
            • something is bound to happen
            • a promise, proposal
            • expresses the posibility of an event to take place
          • gaan:
            • express an intended action
            • to express that an event is going to take place
        • rules:
          • same as present perfect, but hebben/zijn are to the past tense
        • example: *
      5. future:
        • conjugated zullen + infinitive verb
      6. future perfect:
        • usage:
          • something that will have happened in the future
        • conjugated zullen + past participle + hebben/zijn

    • with each tense there are variations for:

      • person(first, second, third)
      • number(singular, plural)
      • mood(active, passive)
      • case(indicative, conditional + 2 more)
